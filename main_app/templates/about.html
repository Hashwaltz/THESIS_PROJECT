About Norzagaray

Norzagaray is a first-class municipality in Bulacan, known for its remarkable balance of nature and modern progress. It was established in 1860 and named after the Spanish Governor-General Fernando Norzagaray y Escudero.

The town boasts significant landmarks such as the Angat Dam, one of the most important water sources for Metro Manila, the Pinagrealan Cave — a historic refuge for revolutionaries, and the Bakas River — a scenic natural retreat.

Today, Norzagaray stands as a model of sustainable local governance. With thriving industries, eco-tourism, and strong leadership, the municipality continues to uphold its commitment to development while preserving its rich cultural roots.

Culture & Heritage

Norzagaray is celebrated for its lively Mayohan Festival, traditional crafts, and agricultural pride. Its people are known for their hospitality, resilience, and dedication to both community and progress.
